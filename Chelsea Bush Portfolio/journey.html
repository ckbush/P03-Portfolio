Journey

A mobile application connecting solo travellers.

Overview

Role: Research, Interaction Design, Prototyping, Testing

Team: Dana Khan, Irene Kim

This is a mobile application for solo travellers that allows them to easily gather information about their travel destination as well as interact with other travellers. The main features of the app consist of splitting costs, sharing tips, messaging other travellers, and arranging group meet-up activities. This project was created for a third year interface design course.

Research & Interviews

My team and I began the project by researching our domain and users. After conducting several interviews, we identified the main problems that solo travellers face to be expenses, meeting others, and safety. With these problems in mind, we began developing our application features to solve these issues.

Prototyping Process

For our solution, we came up with the four features that our app focuses on: splitting transporation and accomodation costs, sharing information on topics such as food or safety, chatting with others, and arranging group meet-ups.

The design process for implementing our solution began with rapid protoyping through sketching and low-fidelity digital wireframes which helped in planning our layout. Choosing the best wireframes, we then went forward to produce high fidelity mockups for each page of the application. Once all pages were complete, we brought them into Flinto where we linked the pages and created micro-interactions to enhance the user experience.

User Testing & Final Stages

During the final stage of the project our team conducted user testing to help identify any problems with our interface. Problems that arose included small font size, unclear icons, and lack of colour contrast. Based on this user testing feedback, we made the necessary changes to our final design.

Lastly, our team took the extra step to create a promotional video to showcase our app. This was not a required component of the project, but we felt it would enhance our presentation when pitching the final idea to our peers.

Reflection

The last stage of this project was testing the website on multiple device sizes to ensure the responsiveness was smooth. After debugging any issues from the device testing, I conducted a content audit as well. Through the content audit I recieved useful feeback from my peers where I was able to fix small issues such as font boldness to increase legibility and hierarchy.
