Journey (2017)

A mobile application connecting solo travellers.

The Application

This is a mobile application for solo travellers that allows them to easily gather information about their travel destination as well as interact with other travellers. The main features of the app consist of splitting accommodation and transportation costs, sharing information on topics such as food or safety, messaging other travellers, and arranging group meet-up activities. This project was created for a third year interface design course.

Research & Interviews

My team and I began the project by researching our domain and users. After conducting several interviews, we identified the main goals of solo travellers to be cost efficiency, meeting others, and ensuring safety while out alone.

With these goals in mind, we began developing our application features to meet these goals.

Prototyping Process

The design process for the interface began with rapid protoyping through sketching and digital wireframes. Choosing the best wireframes, we then went forward to produce high fidelity mockups for each page of the application. Once all pages were complete, we brought them into Flinto where we linked the pages and created micro-interactions to enhance the user experience.

User Testing & Final Stages

During the final stage of the project our team conducted user testing to help identify any problems with our interface. Problems that arose included small font size, unclear icons, and lack of colour contrast. Based on this user testing feedback, we made the necessary changes to our final design.

Lastly, our team took the extra step to create a promotional video to showcase our app. This was not a required component of the project, but we felt it would enhance our presentation when pitching the final idea to our peers.
